What's happening with your water

On the morning of April 9, a pressure drop in the Suffolk County main line created conditions where outside contaminants could have entered the water supply. While testing is ongoing, the county has issued a precautionary boil-water advisory for all residents in the affected zone. This means tap water is likely fine for hand-washing and laundry but should be boiled before drinking, cooking, or brushing teeth. The advisory is expected to be lifted once two consecutive clean test results come back, likely by tomorrow evening.

What to do right now

Boil before you drink

Bring tap water to a rolling boil for at least 1 full minute. Let it cool before use. This applies to drinking, cooking, and making ice.

Do this first

Protect baby formula & meds

Use only boiled or bottled water for infant formula, medication, and any immunocompromised household members. Don't rely on filters alone.

Do this first

Stock 1 gallon per person

Keep at least a gallon of bottled water per person at home. If you can't get to a store, boil and store tap water in clean covered containers.

Important

Pets need safe water too

Swap out pet bowls with boiled or bottled water. Animals are vulnerable to the same waterborne bacteria flagged in this advisory.
